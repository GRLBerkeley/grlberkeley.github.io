Being Queer At Cal

A Few Resources

Academics

  • The Center for the Study of Sexual Cultures was founded in 2001 to support research and critical conversations concerning sexuality, sexual culture, and their mutually determining relationship to institutions, social practices and norms, and modes of representation from a societal and scholarly perspective.

  • The Gender and Women's Studies Department offers interdisciplinary perspectives on the formation of gender and its intersections with other relations of power, such as sexuality, race, class, nationality, religion, and age.

  • The LGBT Studies Minor is an innovative, interdisciplinary undergraduate minor program in Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Transgender (LGBT) Studies. LGBT Studies works to establish sexuality as a crucial category of analysis in the humanities and social sciences.

  • GenSex is a course that focuses on the intersection of gender, sexuality, and identity. Students will have the opportunity to explore aspects of their own identity, and how their experiences have shaped their understanding of their sexuality and self-image.

Housing

  • The UNITY Theme Program is a living-learning community that focuses on LGBTQ+ education, community-building, and leadership development.

  • The Wilde House is part of the Student Cooperative System providing low cost student housing in Berkeley. Wilde welcomes and lavishes affection upon all open minded residents and guests, regardless of sexuality or gender.

    • Resources

  • GENEQ is a UC Berkeley campus community center committed to fostering an inclusive Cal experience for all.

  • The Tang Center clinicians are committed to ensuring that LQBTQ students receive the highest quality medical care in a safe, respectful space.

  • QARC is a student-run organization in charge of providing social, educational, and safe sex programming for the queer and trans community on campus, and also acts as an umbrella organization for all active queer/trans communities on campus.
